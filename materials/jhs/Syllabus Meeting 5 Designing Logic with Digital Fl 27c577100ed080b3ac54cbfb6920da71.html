Topic 2: The Building Blocks: Decoding Flowchart Symbols

Mastering the Symbols of Logic üî∑

Every flowchart uses a standard set of symbols to represent different types of actions or decisions. Think of them as a universal language for logic! Let's learn the main ones we'll be using in Draw.io.

Here‚Äôs a breakdown of the most common symbols you'll find in the "Flowchart" section of Draw.io:

  • Terminator (Oval):¬†Represents the¬†Start¬†and¬†End¬†points of your process. Every flowchart must begin with a "Start" and finish with an "End".
  • Process (Rectangle):¬†This is your main action symbol. It represents a step, a calculation, or an operation. Think of it as a command.
    • Examples:¬†"Turn light green," "Calculate total score," "Save the file."
  • Decision (Diamond):¬†This symbol is crucial for making your flowchart "smart." It asks a "Yes/No" or "True/False" question. It will always have at least two arrows coming out of it: one for the "Yes" path and one for the "No" path.
    • Examples:¬†"Is the timer finished?", "Is the password correct?"
  • Input/Output (Parallelogram):¬†This shape is used whenever the process needs to get information (input) from a user or display information (output) to a screen.
    • Input Example:¬†"Get username from keyboard."
    • Output Example:¬†"Display 'Welcome!' message."
  • Flow Line (Arrow):¬†These are the connectors. They show the direction of the process, guiding you from one step to the next.

How to Use Them in Draw.io:

  1. Click¬†a symbol on the left panel to add it to your canvas.
  1. Double-click¬†inside a shape to type your text.
  1. Hover¬†over a shape until blue arrows appear.¬†Click and drag¬†an arrow to another shape to connect them.

Topic 3: Case Study - The Logic of a Traffic Light

How Do Traffic Lights¬†Really¬†Work? üö¶

A traffic light is the perfect real-world example of the three core logic concepts working together.

  • Sequencing:¬†The lights follow a strict order: Green ‚Üí Yellow ‚Üí Red. This sequence ensures traffic flows safely and predictably.
  • Conditions:¬†The "brain" of the traffic light is always asking a question (a condition). The most common question is based on a timer: "Has the timer for the green light reached 30 seconds?" This is a decision point, perfect for our¬†Diamond¬†symbol.
  • Loops:¬†Does the traffic light cycle once and then go home for the day? No! It¬†repeats¬†its sequence endlessly. This is a¬†loop. In a flowchart, we show a loop by drawing a connector line from the end of the process back to an earlier step.

Topic 4: Flowchart Challenge - Control the Intersection with Draw.io!

Your Mission: Design a Traffic Light Control System üöó

Now it's time to combine your knowledge of logic and your new skills in Draw.io. Your challenge is to create a digital flowchart that maps out the complete process for a traffic light at a simple North/South and East/West intersection.

The Logic You Need to Map:

  1. The process starts.
  1. Turn the North/South (N/S) light¬†Green¬†and the East/West (E/W) light¬†Red.
  1. Wait for 30 seconds.
  1. Turn the N/S light¬†Yellow.
  1. Wait for 5 seconds.
  1. Turn the N/S light¬†Red.
  1. Turn the E/W light¬†Green.
  1. Wait for 30 seconds.
  1. Turn the E/W light¬†Yellow.
  1. Wait for 5 seconds.
  1. Turn the E/W light¬†Red.
  1. Loop!¬†Go back to step 2 to repeat the cycle forever.

Instructions for Draw.io:

  1. Start your flowchart with a "Terminator" (oval) symbol labeled "Start".
  1. Use "Process" (rectangle) symbols for all the actions (like "Turn N/S Light Green" or "Wait 30s").
  1. Connect your symbols in the correct sequence using the arrows.
  1. To create the loop, drag the final arrow from your last step all the way back to the first step of the cycle ("Turn N/S Light Green").
